Koffer

Transforming a space within Golden Mile Complex into a flexible multi-purpose environment that brings together retail, recreation, gathering and community interaction.

Branding Space Planning Experience
Role
Interior Designer, Researcher
Year
2020
Type
Diploma Final-Year Project
Tools
AutoCAD, 3Ds Max, Photoshop
Deliverables
Spatial Concept, Space Planning, 3D Visualisation, Material Development and Presentation

The Challenge

Golden Mile Complex contained a mix of communities, businesses and social activity, but many areas lacked flexible spaces where different users could gather, participate and spend time together. The challenge was to create an environment that supported multiple functions without feeling fragmented.

The Approach

I developed a layered spatial system combining communal seating, retail displays, dining areas, activity zones and open circulation. Warm timber, modular furnishings and flexible zones were used to create a welcoming experience that could adapt to different users and activities.

03 — Spatial Showcase

Spatial Highlights

Since this earlier project is mainly documented through final visualisations, the space is presented through seven key perspectives that show the retail, communal and activity zones.

Project Highlights

Community-Centered Space

Encourages interaction and connection through shared seating and open zones.

Flexible & Adaptive

Zoned for multiple functions with movable furniture and clear circulation.

Retail & Lifestyle Integration

Retail displays and dining experiences coexist with comfort and flow.

Material & Experience

A balanced palette of warm timber, concrete textures and green accents creates a calm, inviting atmosphere. Natural materials and soft lighting enhance comfort while supporting a modern and approachable identity.

☼

Design Intent

  • Create a flexible social hub where retail, leisure and community can coexist.
  • Encourage interaction and communal activity through shared seating and open zones.
  • Design spaces that are welcoming, adaptive and experience-led.

Outcome & Reflection

Koffer became a flexible communal environment where retail, leisure and social activity could coexist. The project taught me to balance circulation, function and atmosphere while designing for different user needs. It also shaped the spatial and experience-led thinking that continues to influence my graphic and brand design work today.
