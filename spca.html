SPCA
Rebrand

Reframing SPCA Singapore’s identity to communicate compassion, trust and community while encouraging stronger public participation in animal welfare.

Branding Campaign Experience
Role
Designer, Researcher
Year

Type

The Challenge

Animal shelters are often communicated through sadness and urgency, which can cause audiences to disengage. The challenge was to create an identity that still conveyed the seriousness of animal welfare while feeling hopeful, welcoming and community-driven.

The Approach

I developed a warmer and more approachable visual system centred on compassion, trust and collective action. The identity was extended across campaign materials, social content, postcards and a physical booth experience to encourage participation beyond passive awareness.

Process & Exploration

The rebrand developed through research, identity exploration, campaign applications and a booth experience. Select a chapter to view the process in greater detail.

Outcome & Reflection

The final system presents SPCA as both a trusted organisation and a community people can actively participate in. By shifting the tone from guilt-led communication towards warmth and shared responsibility, the project showed how branding can make animal welfare feel more approachable without weakening its importance.
